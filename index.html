Legal & judicial reference

Context on the structure of the German judiciary and where to find official EU and national performance data. Content is provided for general information only.

The German court system is organised in a federal structure. The ordinary civil and criminal courts run from local and regional courts (Amts- und Landgerichte) through higher regional courts (Oberlandesgerichte) to the Federal Court of Justice (Bundesgerichtshof) for final review. The Federal Constitutional Court (Bundesverfassungsgericht) decides matters under the Grundgesetz (Basic Law).

International comparisons of justice systems use indicators such as efficiency, quality, and independence. The following sources are maintained by public bodies or intergovernmental organisations; figures and country rankings are updated on their own publication cycles.

  • EU Justice Scoreboard (European Commission) — annual comparative data (efficiency, quality, independence).
  • CEPEJ (Council of Europe) — European Commission for the Efficiency of Justice: methodology and country reports.
  • Bundesministerium der Justiz — German Federal Ministry of Justice and Consumer Protection.
  • Bundesverfassungsgericht — German Federal Constitutional Court.

This overview does not constitute legal advice. For binding information, consult qualified counsel and the official sources linked above.
