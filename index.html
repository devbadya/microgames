Justiz in Deutschland (overview)

German courts are organized federally: the ordinary system splits into local and regional courts (Amts- und Landgerichte), then higher regional courts (Oberlandesgerichte) and, for civil and criminal review, the Federal Court of Justice (Bundesgerichtshof). The Federal Constitutional Court (Bundesverfassungsgericht) decides questions of the Basic Law (Grundgesetz).

Performance of justice systems is compared internationally with rule-of-law and efficiency indicators. Useful starting points (external, official or widely cited):

  • EU Justice Scoreboard (European Commission) — annual country comparisons (efficiency, quality, independence of justice).
  • CEPEJ (Council of Europe) — European Commission for the Efficiency of Justice: statistics and reports.
  • Bundesministerium der Justiz — federal justice ministry (policy, legislation, services).
  • Bundesverfassungsgericht — Germany’s Federal Constitutional Court.

This page is a general summary for visitors from Germany. It is not legal advice. Figures and rankings change year to year; use the links above for current reports.
